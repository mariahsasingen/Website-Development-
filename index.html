What Is Online Learning?

Online learning, which can also be said as eLearning, is one of the teaching and learning techniques where the internet is the main component here. It gathers the students and their teachers together through software platforms that allow them to communicate, face, share, and interact with each other by using the technology devices, such as mobile phones, laptops, computers, tablets, and other considerable devices. Furthermore, online learning is a form of distance learning in which education makes use of internet technology enhancement.

IMPORTANCE OF ONLINE LEARNING

Allows students to learn anytime, students can repeat the video recordings of their classes and the materials used in lessons anytime they need. They can also adjust their learning speed according to their certain abilities.

It ensures a dynamic interaction between teachers and students. Online learning can build dynamic interactions between teachers and students as well as students and their fellow students.

It allows high-quality discussions. The structure of online discussion allows students to think and reflect on the questions and feedback from teachers or other students before replying.

It ensures easy access to learning resources. Conventional learning models require students to hold certain books. Unlike the traditional method, online distance learning allows teachers to upload all learning materials into a learning platform so students can easily access them at any time.

Advantages and Disadvantages of eLearning
ADVANTAGES  DISADVANTAGES
Efficiency

Teachers can effectively give lessons to students through online learning. Teachers can use all of the resources available for online learning, including podcasts, PDFs, and videos, as part of their lesson preparations.

Inability to Focus on Screen

One of the largest difficulties of online learning for many students is their inability to maintain their attention for extended periods of time.

Accessibility of Time and Place

The ability for students to attend classes from any location of their choosing is another benefit of online education.

Technology Issues

Internet connectivity is another significant issue for online learning.

Affordable

Reduced financial expenditures are another benefit of online education.

Sense of Isolation

Being around peers allows for a wealth of learning opportunities for students.

Improve Student Attendance

There are lower risks of pupils missing lectures because online courses can be attended from home or any other location.

Poor Teacher Training

Teachers who use online learning must have a fundamental understanding of using digital learning tools.

Suits a Variety of Learning Styles

Every scholar has a unique learning path and a different learning style.

Poor Screen Time Management

Many parents are worried about the health risks associated with letting their kids spend so much time staring at screens.

"eLearning is a form of distance education that allows teachers and students to connect through online platforms. It provides flexibility and is essential during times of crisis, such as the COVID-19 pandemic. The history of eLearning dates back to 1953, and it has continued to grow over the years. Various online platforms such as Microsoft Teams, Google Classroom, and Zoom are available for eLearning. eLearning offers many advantages, such as flexibility and access to learning resources, but it also has disadvantages, including distractions and technology issues."
